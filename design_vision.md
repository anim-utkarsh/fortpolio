# Design Vision: The Dig-Down Zine

Since we're holding off on code, let's "paint" the two extremes of our journey.

## 🌤️ Layer 1: The Surface (The Modern Era)
**Vibe**: Sleek, high-end, premium tech.
- **Background**: Deep charcoal grey with a subtle "digital grain" texture.
- **Main Element**: A large, glowing "leaf" of paper that acts as the container for your Rive/Figma work. It has a slight **glassmorphism** effect—frosted edges with neon-blue backlighting.
- **Typography**: Bold, clean sans-serif (Inter or Outfit). 
- **Interaction**: A Rive-animated cursor that reacts to proximity to buttons. When you hover, the "leaf" tilts slightly in 3D (GSAP parallax).

## 🕳️ Layer 7: The Bedrock (The 2003 Era)
**Vibe**: Nostalgic, clunky, but charming "PowerPoint" aesthetic.
- **Background**: A literal dirt/soil texture. Embedded in the soil are "fossilized" tech: a 3.5-inch floppy disk, a Clipart sun, and a CD-ROM.
- **Main Element**: A "leaf" of yellowing notebook paper or an old PowerPoint slide (complete with the "Gradient Fill" background). It’s held in place by a digital "push pin" or "sticky tape."
- **Typography**: Arial or Comic Sans (for the real retro feel).
- **Interaction**: The scroll feel becomes "heavier." Buttons have that classic Windows XP bevel/emboss effect and make a "click" sound when pressed.

## 🎞️ The Transition
As you scroll between these, the **"Leaf Carrier"** (the portfolio case) isn't seen, but its *presence* is felt. You are essentially digging through the archives of that case.
- **Surface**: The paper is crisp and white.
- **Middle Earth**: The paper gets dog-eared, slightly yellowed, and maybe has some Photoshop CS3-style flares.
- **Bedrock**: The paper is tattered and "fossilized" into the dirt.
